Coffee Shop Sales Performance Analysis

Project Overview
This project analyzes transactional data from a coffee shop to uncover insights into sales performance, product demand, and customer purchasing patterns. The goal is to support data-driven decision-making by identifying trends across time, product categories, and store locations.

Objectives
- Analyze overall sales performance and revenue trends
- Identify top 15 performing products and categories
- Evaluate store location performance
- Understand customer purchasing behavior across different times of the day
- Provide actionable recommendations to improve business performance

Dataset Description
The dataset contains transaction-level records with the following fields:
Transaction ID, Transaction Date, Transaction Time, Transaction Quantity, Store ID, Store Location, Product ID, Unit Price, Product Category, Product Type, Product Details

Tools Used
- Excel
- Data Cleaning
- Pivot Tables / DAX
- Data Visualization

Key Metrics (KPIs)
- Total Revenue = Quantity × Unit Price
- Total Transactions


Analysis & Insights

Sales Performance
Revenue trends show fluctuations across different periods, with peak sales occurring during high-traffic hours.

Product Performance
Barista Espresso and Brewed Chai Tea product categories contributed significantly to revenue growth due to higher demand, while some products underperform.

Store Performance
Sales vary across store locations, indicating differences in customer traffic and demand. Stores in Hell's Kitchen attract more customers

Time-Based Analysis
Peak sales hours occur during morning and evening periods. Certain days like Thursday, Friday and Monday show increased transaction volume.

Key Insights
- A small number of products contribute most of the revenue
- Customer demand is time-dependent
- Store location impacts performance

Recommendations
- Increase staff during busy period, like days like Thurday, Friday and Monday by preparing high demand products in advance
- There should be a focus on high performing products by promoting best selling products and bundles them with other products
- Applying successful strategies from top stores to weak stores
